What is a Tube Filling Machine?

A tube filling machine is specialized equipment designed to accurately fill and seal tubes with products such as creams, gels, ointments, pastes, or semi-liquids. These machines are widely used in the cosmetic, pharmaceutical, chemical, and food industries to ensure consistent filling, secure sealing, and hygienic processing.

The working principle of tube filling machines involves automatic or semi-automatic feeding of empty tubes, precise dosing of the product, and reliable sealing—either by heat, crimping, or ultrasonic methods—followed by batch coding and trimming. This ensures high efficiency, reduced wastage, and professional packaging quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between rotary and linear tube filling machines?

Rotary tube filling machines use a rotating indexing table, which allows for high-speed production and precise positioning. Linear machines operate in a straight-line motion, making them ideal for medium-speed production and easier maintenance.

How do I choose the right filling capacity?

Your choice depends on production requirements. For small batches, a capacity of 20–60 tubes/min is sufficient. For large-scale production, machines like the RTFS-120, which can handle 7200 pcs/hr, are more suitable.

Can these machines handle both plastic and aluminum tubes?

Yes, most modern tube filling machines are compatible with both plastic and aluminum tubes. However, sealing mechanisms may differ, so it’s important to select a model that supports your preferred packaging type.

What is the typical lead time for delivery?

Lead time varies by model and customization requirements, but most machines are delivered within 4–8 weeks after order confirmation.
